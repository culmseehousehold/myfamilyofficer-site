# 📂 STRATEGY DOSSIER: The "Cinematic" Protocol
**Decision:** Pivot from "Interactive Simulation" to "High-Fidelity Product Cinema"
**Date:** March 21, 2026
**Status:** 🚀 APPROVED

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**The Strategic Pivot:** We have moved away from the "Interactive Glass Box" (browser-based simulation) due to complexity and uncanny valley risks. We are returning to **Core Principles**: showing the product via **High-Fidelity Video Storytelling**.

**The New Approach:** "Show, Don't Touch."
Instead of asking users to learn a fake interface, we will curate perfect, cinematic journeys of the real application performing complex tasks. This controls the narrative completely while still proving capability.

---

## 2. The Implementation: "Product Tour" Video Series

We will produce three key assets to replace the interactive demo:

### Asset A: The "Hero" Loop (Homepage)
*   **Format:** Autoplay, muted, high-res video in the Hero section.
*   **Content:** Fast cuts of the most impressive UI interactions (Sankey diagrams populating, Agents scanning PDFs, Dark Mode toggles).
*   **Goal:** Immediate visual authority. "This looks expensive and powerful."

### Asset B: The "Compliance" Deep Dive (Feature Section)
*   **Format:** 45-second narrated walkthrough.
*   **Script:** "Watch FamilyOfficer detect a Div 7A violation in real-time."
*   **Visuals:** Split screen showing a PDF trust deed on the left and the Agent extracting clauses on the right.

### Asset C: The "Principals" Overview
*   **Format:** 60-second "Boardroom Ready" tour.
*   **Content:** Aggregated Net Worth across multiple entities. Focusing on the "Source of Truth" value prop.

---

## 3. Rationale for the Pivot

1.  **Narrative Control:** An interactive demo allows users to click "boring" things or get lost. A video forces them to see the "Aha!" moments in the correct order.
2.  **Maintenance:** Maintaining a separate "Headless" React app () that duplicates the main app's UI is technical debt. Videos are static assets.
3.  **Realism:** We can record the *actual* dev environment with full backend power, rather than faking it with client-side JSON.
